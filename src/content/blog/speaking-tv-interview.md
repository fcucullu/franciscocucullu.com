I was invited to appear on the "Malaga Tech" segment of Hola Andalucia, a program on 101 TV Malaga. The segment focused on technology innovation and entrepreneurship in the region, and I had the opportunity to discuss what it takes to build products in a fast-moving industry.

Malaga has grown into one of Europe's most dynamic tech hubs. Companies like Google, Vodafone, and dozens of startups have set up operations in the city, drawn by talent, infrastructure, and quality of life. Being featured on local television to talk about this ecosystem was a great way to connect with the broader community.

The conversation covered how technology startups approach product development, what makes Malaga attractive for founders, and how AI is changing the way companies operate. It was a concise format that forced clarity, which is always a useful exercise.

Television appearances like this are valuable not just for visibility, but for practicing the skill of communicating complex ideas simply. When you have a few minutes on camera, every word counts.
