I joined a radio program to discuss technology, entrepreneurship, and innovation. Radio interviews have a unique format: there is no visual aid, no slides, no body language to lean on. Everything depends on how clearly you can explain your ideas with words alone.

The conversation covered how technology is reshaping industries, what it means to be an entrepreneur in the current landscape, and how AI is creating new opportunities for builders. Radio audiences tend to be broader than typical tech events, which means you need to make technical concepts accessible without oversimplifying them.

One topic that came up was the difference between innovation for its own sake and innovation that solves real problems. It is easy to get excited about new technology, but the founders who succeed are the ones who stay focused on user pain points rather than chasing the latest trend.

Radio remains an underrated medium for reaching people who are not in the tech bubble. Many business owners, professionals, and potential users listen to radio during their commute or workday. Being able to communicate your vision in that format is a skill worth developing.
