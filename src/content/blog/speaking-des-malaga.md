The Digital Enterprise Show (DES) is one of Europe's largest events focused on digital transformation. It brings together tech leaders, investors, and enterprise decision-makers from across the continent. I pitched FlexRent to investors at the Malaga edition, competing alongside startups from multiple countries.

DES is different from typical startup conferences. The audience skews toward enterprise buyers and corporate innovation teams, not just VCs and angels. That changes the pitch dynamic. Instead of focusing purely on growth metrics and fundraising narratives, you need to speak the language of enterprise value: cost reduction, operational efficiency, and scalable integration.

Pitching FlexRent in this context meant framing the product not just as a consumer-facing solution, but as a platform that could integrate into larger enterprise ecosystems. The feedback from enterprise decision-makers was different from what you hear at a typical demo day, and that perspective was invaluable for shaping the product roadmap.

Events like DES remind you that startups do not exist in a vacuum. The best products solve problems that matter to large organizations, and the founders who understand enterprise needs have a significant advantage when it comes to scaling.
