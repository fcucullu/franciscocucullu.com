We discussed how product vision evolves over time. The initial idea is rarely the one that succeeds. What matters is the ability to listen to users, read the data honestly, and adapt without losing sight of the core problem you set out to solve. Too many founders fall in love with their solution instead of the problem.

The interview also covered execution. Having a great idea is the easy part. The hard part is shipping consistently, making decisions with incomplete information, and maintaining momentum when things are not going well. These are the skills that separate founders who build lasting companies from those who do not.

Long-form interviews like this one are valuable because they allow for nuance. A pitch deck gives you three minutes to tell a story. An interview gives you space to explain the reasoning behind your decisions and share the lessons that only come from experience.
